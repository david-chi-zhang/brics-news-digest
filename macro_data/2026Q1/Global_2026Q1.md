## International Trade
### 2026-03
**WTO 2026 Global Trade Outlook (Released: 19 March 2026)**

**2026 Trade Growth Scenarios:**
- **Baseline Scenario:** Merchandise trade growth 1.9% in 2026 (down from 4.6% in 2025); Services trade growth 4.8% in 2026 (down from 5.3% in 2025); Combined goods and services trade growth 2.7% in 2026 (vs 4.7% in 2025)
- **High Energy Price Scenario** (if Middle East conflict persists): Merchandise trade growth 1.4% in 2026 (-0.5pp from baseline); Services trade growth 4.1% in 2026 (-0.7pp from baseline); GDP forecast reduced by 0.3pp
- **Upside Potential:** If conflict is short-lived and AI spending remains strong, merchandise trade could reach 2.4% in 2026 (+0.5pp from baseline)

**WTO Director-General Ngozi Okonjo-Iweala Statement:**
*"The outlook reflects the resilience of global trade, buoyed by trade in high technology products and digitally delivered services, adaptations in supply chains and the avoidance of tit-for-tat retaliation on tariffs. However, this baseline forecast is under pressure from the conflict in the Middle East. Sustained increases in energy prices could increase risks for global trade, with potential spillovers for food security and cost pressures on consumers and businesses. Nevertheless, WTO members can help cushion the impact and ease the economic burden on people worldwide by maintaining predictable trade policies and strengthening supply chain resilience."*

**2025 Trade Performance (Exceeding Expectations - Actual 4.6% vs Predicted 2.4%):**
- **Tariff Policy Adjustments:** Suspension of new US tariffs until August 2025; Limited retaliation from other economies; Numerous tariff exemptions
- **AI-Enabling Products Surge:** Trade in AI-enabling goods increased 21.9% YoY to US$ 4.18 trillion (from US$ 3.43T in 2024); Accounted for 42% of total global trade growth despite representing only 1/6 of global trade; Key products (chips, semiconductors, data transmission equipment) exempt from most new tariffs
- **MFN Framework:** 72% of world trade conducted on most-favoured-nation basis by end of February 2026

**Regional Projections 2026 (Baseline - Merchandise Imports):**
- Asia: 3.3% (fastest) | Africa: 3.2% | South America: 2.5% | Europe: 1.3% | Middle East: 1.0% | North America: 0.3% (flat) | CIS: -2.0% (contraction)

**Key Risks:**
- Middle East conflict affecting Strait of Hormuz (traffic collapsed from 138 vessels/day to almost zero)
- Fertilizer supply disruptions (1/3 of world's fertilizer exports pass through Strait)
- Over 40,000 flights cancelled; increased transport and insurance costs
